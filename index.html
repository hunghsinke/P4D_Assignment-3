Aboriginal Places in the ACT

We acknowledges the First Peoples of this land and recognises their continuous connection to culture, community and Country.

Recognising the Traditional Owners' custodianship of the land is a fundamental act of respect, affirming the enduring connection of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples to the land, their forebears, and cultural heritage. This gesture of respect is open to individuals from both Indigenous and non-Indigenous backgrounds.

Only the traditional owners can speak for and welcome visitors to Country or give others the authority to do so.

-- The Australian Institute of Aboriginal and Torres Strait islander Studies (AIATSIS).

Welcome to Ngunnawal Country

About Us

Welcome to our website dedicated to Aboriginal places, a platform that celebrates the rich cultural heritage and profound history of Indigenous culture in ACT. As we embark on this journey of exploration and learning, we begin by acknowledging and paying our deepest respects to the Elders, both past and present, who have been the custodians of these lands for countless generations.

Our mission is to provide a digital space where you can discover, understand, and appreciate the diverse and profound importance of these places. Through articles, stories, images, and interactive resources, we aim to foster a greater understanding of the significance of Aboriginal places in our shared human history and the ongoing efforts to protect and preserve them.

Sacred Trees

Culturally, scarred trees were created when Aboriginal people removed bark for a variety of purposes such as making canoes, shields and containers.
